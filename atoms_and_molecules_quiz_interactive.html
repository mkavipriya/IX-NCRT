Atoms and Molecules - MCQ Quiz

1. The smallest particle of an element that retains its chemical properties is:

a) Molecule
b) Atom
c) Ion
d) Compound
Correct Answer: b) Atom. An atom is the smallest unit of an element that retains its chemical properties.

2. Who proposed the atomic theory?

a) Dalton
b) Thomson
c) Rutherford
d) Bohr
Correct Answer: a) Dalton. John Dalton proposed the atomic theory in 1808.

3. The chemical symbol for Sodium is:

a) So
b) Na
c) Sd
d) Sn
Correct Answer: b) Na. The symbol 'Na' comes from Latin word 'Natrium'.

4. Which of these is a diatomic molecule?

a) CO₂
b) O₂
c) H₂O
d) CH₄
Correct Answer: b) O₂. O₂ is a diatomic molecule consisting of two oxygen atoms.

5. The SI unit of amount of substance is:

a) Mole
b) Gram
c) Kilogram
d) Liter
Correct Answer: a) Mole. The mole is the SI unit for amount of substance.

6. The atomic number of Carbon is:

a) 6
b) 12
c) 8
d) 4
Correct Answer: a) 6. Carbon has atomic number 6.

7. Which of these represents Avogadro’s number?

a) 6.022 × 10²³
b) 3.14 × 10²²
c) 9.11 × 10⁻³¹
d) 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹
Correct Answer: a) 6.022 × 10²³. Avogadro's number is 6.022 × 10²³ particles per mole.

8. The formula of water is:

a) HO
b) H₂O
c) OH₂
d) H₂O₂
Correct Answer: b) H₂O. Water's chemical formula is H₂O.

9. Which of these is a triatomic molecule?

a) O₂
b) CO₂
c) N₂
d) H₂
Correct Answer: b) CO₂. CO₂ has three atoms: one carbon and two oxygen.

10. The mass of one mole of oxygen atoms is:

a) 8 g
b) 16 g
c) 32 g
d) 18 g
Correct Answer: b) 16 g. One mole of oxygen atoms weighs 16 grams.

11. Which of these is a polyatomic ion?

a) Na⁺
b) Cl⁻
c) SO₄²⁻
d) K⁺
Correct Answer: c) SO₄²⁻. SO₄²⁻ (sulfate) is a polyatomic ion.

12. The valency of Nitrogen in NH₃ is:

a) 1
b) 2
c) 3
d) 4
Correct Answer: c) 3. Nitrogen forms three bonds in NH₃, so valency is 3.

13. Which law states that ‘In a chemical reaction, mass is neither created nor destroyed’:

a) Law of Constant Proportion
b) Law of Conservation of Mass
c) Law of Multiple Proportion
d) Law of Definite Composition
Correct Answer: b) Law of Conservation of Mass. Law of Conservation of Mass states this principle.

14. The molecular mass of CO₂ is:

a) 28 u
b) 32 u
c) 44 u
d) 18 u
Correct Answer: c) 44 u. CO₂ has molecular mass of 44 u.

15. Which of these is an example of a molecule of an element?

a) H₂O
b) CO₂
c) O₂
d) NaCl
Correct Answer: c) O₂. O₂ is a molecule made of only one element: oxygen.

16. The charge on an electron is:

a) +1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
b) –1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
c) 0
d) +1 C
Correct Answer: b) –1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C. Electron has a negative charge of –1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C.

17. Which of these is not a part of Dalton’s atomic theory?

a) Atoms are indivisible
b) Atoms of same element are identical
c) Atoms can be created or destroyed
d) Atoms combine in whole number ratios
Correct Answer: c) Atoms can be created or destroyed. Dalton stated atoms cannot be created or destroyed.

18. The number of atoms in 1 mole of hydrogen gas (H₂) is:

a) 6.022 × 10²³
b) 3.011 × 10²³
c) 1.204 × 10²⁴
d) 6.022 × 10²²
Correct Answer: c) 1.204 × 10²⁴. One mole of H₂ molecules has 2 atoms per molecule, so 1.204 × 10²⁴ atoms.

19. Which of these is the correct chemical formula for Ammonia?

a) NH₃
b) NH₄
c) NO₃
d) N₂H₄
Correct Answer: a) NH₃. Ammonia's formula is NH₃.

20. The relative mass of an atom compared to hydrogen is called:

a) Atomic number
b) Atomic mass
c) Molecular mass
d) Valency
Correct Answer: b) Atomic mass. Atomic mass is the relative mass compared to hydrogen.
